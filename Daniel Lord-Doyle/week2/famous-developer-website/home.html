Who was Mary-Lee Berners-Lee?

Mary Lee Berners-Lee (née Woods; 12 March 1924 – 29 November 2017) was an English mathematician and computer scientist who worked in a team that developed programs in the Department of Computer Science, University of Manchester Mark 1, Ferranti Mark 1 and Mark 1 Star computers. She was the mother of Sir Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the World Wide Web and Mike Berners-Lee, an English researcher and writer on greenhouse gases.

There's Something About Mary...

Fun fact #1 - Mary also moved to Australia for a few years!

Fun fact #2 - While at Ferranti, Woods discovered that the women in her department were getting less pay than the men. She presented the case to the personnel department and was able to convince them to grant equal pay and rights for women.

Fun fact #3 - She did her own MTA project! She did some work with the London Transport Executive, to develop a simulation for bus routes that could prevent hold ups and bus bunching.

Fun fact #4 - She invented freelance software enginnering! She termed it "cottage industry programming," so that she could complete jobs from home.
